Relearning the Foundations

Posted:

Every high-performing organization has one thing in common: strong fundamentals. Whether you’re running a company, managing a team, or building technology — your decisions are only as good as the data behind them. This week, I’m revisiting the foundational tools that power modern operations and intelligent decision-making:

  • Excel lookups (VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, Index/Match)
  • Excel macros & automation
  • Visual Basic for Applications (VBA)
  • SQL for structured data extraction

These aren’t “beginner tools.” They’re core instruments used every day across Fortune 500 analytics teams, finance departments, logistics hubs, and enterprise reporting systems. Relearning them isn’t about going backwards — it’s about strengthening the base that everything else sits on.

Excel Lookups: The First Layer of Insight

Lookups are the fastest way to transform raw information into actionable data. They enable matching, merging, and verifying information without manual review — critical in environments where accuracy and speed matter.

Macros & VBA: Automating Operational Burden

Any executive knows the real cost inside an organization isn’t the one-time task — it’s the tasks repeated thousands of times. Macros turn repeatable processes into automated workflows, eliminating human error and unlocking hours of productivity.

VBA is the logic that powers those macros. It’s not glamorous, but it is effective. Years ago, I used VBA to automatically create individual data sheets for every associate in a large dataset — a task that would have taken hours by hand. Code did it in seconds.

SQL: Precision Data Extraction

SQL is where data stops being static and becomes strategic. It’s the foundation of enterprise reporting, compliance checks, financial forecasting, customer analytics — everything that keeps a large business moving.

I explained it to a friend recently using an example he’d recognize:

“Imagine you work at a major logistics hub — something on the scale of a FedEx facility in Dallas–Fort Worth. Now imagine needing to pull every order that has handled over the past two years.

If you attempted it manually, you’d be reviewing thousands of records across different shifts, managers, and systems. SQL retrieves it instantly — clean, filtered, and ready for analysis.”

That’s the power of fundamentals: Excel handles exploration, VBA automates the repetitive tasks, and SQL delivers precision at scale.

Strong companies are built on strong systems. Strong systems are built on strong fundamentals. This week is about sharpening those fundamentals — so every layer above them stays solid.
